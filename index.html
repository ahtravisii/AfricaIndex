Democracy & Sovereignty
Index — Africa 2024
This platform tests one assumption: that scoring well on Western democracy indices reflects genuine democratic quality. It adds two dimensions no standard index measures.
Procedural Democracy
Elections, civil liberties, press freedom. The EIU/V-Dem framework — useful, but only one part of the picture.
Citizen Legitimacy
What citizens actually experience — trust, accountability, economic delivery. From Afrobarometer surveys.
Sovereignty Score
Economic and political autonomy — CFA franc, IMF programmes, foreign military, resource control. A constructed proxy — not a direct measurement.

⚖ How to use Bias Tests
Standard democracy indices only measure procedural factors — elections, civil liberties, press freedom. This tab asks: what happens to country rankings if we also count whether citizens feel their government is legitimate, or whether a country controls its own economy?
Click a preset — each applies different weights. "Procedural-only" replicates what standard indices measure. The others add legitimacy and sovereignty to the ranking formula.
"Most overrated" = countries that rank high on standard indices but fall when citizen experience or sovereignty is counted. May indicate dependency being mistaken for democracy.
"Most underrated" = countries penalised by procedural-only scoring whose citizens report higher satisfaction or who exercise genuine autonomy.
The shift number shows how many ranking places a country moves vs. procedural-only. Green = rises, red = falls. A large shift means the standard index may be misleading.

Sovereignty Score — Constructed Proxy Index
The original contribution of this platform. A constructed index using five proxies — not a direct measurement. Designed for comparative interpretation, not as a definitive truth claim.
Currency independence (CFA franc membership reduces score)
IMF programme conditionality history (active programme reduces score)
Foreign military bases on territory (presence reduces score)
Resource contract terms (foreign-controlled revenues reduce score)
Aid dependency ratio (ODA as % GNI reduces score at high levels)
Data Sources
Procedural: EIU Democracy Index 2023, V-Dem Country Data v13, Freedom House Freedom in the World 2023.

Legitimacy: Afrobarometer Round 9 (2021–2023). Not all countries surveyed in the same year; some estimates draw from Round 8. Survey perception data has known sampling limitations.

Sovereignty: IMF Country Information pages, SIPRI Military Expenditure Database, EITI Extractive Industries Transparency Initiative, World Bank ODA data (DT.ODA.ODAT.GN.ZS).
Limitations & Honest Disclaimers Sovereignty is proxy-based and constructed — not directly measured. Legitimacy depends on survey perception data with known sampling and timing limitations. Cross-country comparability is imperfect due to different data vintages. Scores should be treated as indicative ranges, not precise measurements. Interpretive narratives are analytical summaries, not raw data claims. This platform tests a hypothesis; it does not prove it.
Central Hypothesis (investigative framing): If sovereignty-asserting states consistently cluster at the bottom of procedural rankings while Western-dependent states cluster at the top — regardless of citizen experience — this may suggest existing indices measure compliance with a particular political economy rather than democratic quality. The live correlation in the header tests this claim. It does not settle it.
